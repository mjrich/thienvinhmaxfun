Our Story

Our Story

ThienVinh and Max met on a fateful Halloween (October 31st 2015) in Washington, DC. Max was dressed as his favourite fruit—the avocado—and had bountiful candy bars hidden in the belly of his jacket. ThienVinh was dressed as colourful peacock was often mistaken as a fairy, after making her costume from a crafts and scraps non-profit shop. ThienVinh and Max had to wait a month for their first date, given competing travel schedules to Nairobi, London, Berlin and different parts of California.

Max asked ThienVinh to be his life partner in her parents’ backyard in Sacramento in the summer of 2017. ThienVinh didn’t immediate answer him; instead, she suggested that they take a walk along the Sacramento River. There, she made him a ring out of a daisy and asked him to be her life partner. Max said yes and then ThienVinh also said yes when Max presented her with a ring from his pocket.

Since then, ThienVinh and Max have travelled to many places together while also nesting in Washington, DC. Adventures include witnessing the Northern Lights in Iceland; eating Turkish food on Christmas Day in East London; walking the 20-mile “Wivey Way” in the British countryside; whale-watching in Baja Mexico; seeing tropical birds in Trinidad and Tobago; riding bikes in New Orleans; and eating tacos in Berlin made by Maya, Max’s sister.

Their most ambitious trip to date was a trip to Asia, where Max and ThienVinh held a “Đám Hỏi” engagement ceremony in Vietnam with their families and friends in the town of Châu Đốc, where ThienVinh was born. During this trip in August 2018, Max’s and ThienVinh’s parents and immediate family met and got to know each for the first time, while Max met ThienVinh’s extended family including six generations of her ancestors. Afterwards, they sailed away on the Mekong River to Cambodia en route to Taiwan and Japan.

ThienVinh and Max are delighted to extend their celebrations with their community of family and friends at their wedding on the American River in Lotus, California!

Getting there

Our venue, Earthtrek Expeditions, is located in Lotus, California, which is about 50 miles east of Sacramento. It will take about 1 hour to drive from Sacramento to Lotus, California. There will be plenty of parking available on-site.

For those flying in, the best airport is Sacramento International Airport (SMF), which is about a 15 minute drive away from downtown Sacramento and an hour drive from Lotus, California. Oakland International Airport (OAK) and San Francisco International Airport (SFO) are both about 1.5 hours away from Sacramento and 2.5 hours from Lotus.

For those taking the bus or train, Sacramento has Amtrak (train), Greyhound bus and Megabus stations. It is also has Lyft and Uber.

At the moment, we suggest that friends get to Northern California (to the Bay Area or Sacramento) and carpool to the site, either by riding with local friends with cars or renting your own car. We are considering booking shuttles between Sacramento and Lotus. We will message everyone and update this website if we do end up offering shuttles or if we decide to try to matchmake drivers and passengers.

Tent Cabin Glamping Goodness

We’ve reserved 150 bunk beds in tent cabins at the wedding venue. These are provided at no cost. Each tent sleeps 2 to 12 and we will be assigning people to bunks. Our wedding will also be kid-friendly, though please keep a close eye on them as we are on the river. Please bring your own bedding, otherwise you can rent a sleeping bag for $15 and a pillow for $5 or the whole weekend. You can also pitch your own tent on the venue site as well. If you prefer to stay elsewhere, there are also B&Bs and hotels in the area. We’d love for you to take advantage of the tent cabins, but do let us know if there are ways we can help ensure the tent cabins or other nearby accommodations allow you to fully enjoy the party.

Food and Drink in the Area

Please plan on dining out after you check-in on Friday evening. There are several restaurants and pubs located in very close proximity to the venue. We will be providing breakfast and then dinner on Saturday. Please arrange your own lunches on Saturday. We will also be providing breakfast before for those departing on Sunday. There will be snacks, water, non-alcoholic and alcoholic beverages available throughout the three days. We aim to cater to different dietary needs and restrictions, including to those who eat vegan, gluten-free, and nut-free foods. Please find below a map and recommended local restaurants (courtesy of Earthtrek Expeditions).

Squally's On the River

Located right next door to our campground, Squally's On the River is a restaurant serving beers, pizza, BBQ, burgers, and salads. Many fresh ingredients are sourced from local farms, ranches, orchards, breweries, and vineyards.

Squally's On the River

Marcos Cafe

Located just a short walk, Marcos Cafe is an outdoor family-style restaurant serving unique pizzas, salads, fresh appetizers, and local beer and wine. Local live music can also be enjoyed on weekends.

Marcos Cafe

Sierra Rizing Cafe and Bakery

Located in the same parking lot as our campground, Sierra Rizing Cafe and Bakery, is favorite amongst locals and travelers alike. Enjoy a variety of coffee and tea beverages as well as delicious home-baked goods.

Sierra Rizing Cafe and Bakery

Riyo Yogurt

Located in the same parking lot as our campground, Riyo Yogurt, is a self-serve frozen yogurt with delicious flavors (dairy-free as well) and toppings bar that is guaranteed you cool you down on a hot summer day.

Riyo Yogurt

The River Shack Deli and Pub

Located about a quarter of a mile from our campground. The River Shack features made-to-order deli sandwiches and a wide variety of beers. The perfect spot to hang out and live life on river time.

Coloma Club Bar and Restaurant
